The Ozow Pay-in API gives you a straightforward way to accept EFT payments from South African customers. You post a payment request to Ozow, redirect your customer to the secure Ozow payment gateway where they complete the transaction through their bank, and then receive the outcome both as a redirect back to your site and as a server-to-server webhook notification. The entire flow is designed around three clear steps that you can wire into any server-side stack with minimal ceremony.

## How it works

The Pay-in API follows a three-step flow:

1. **POST the payment** — Your server sends a signed payment request to `/PostPaymentRequest`. Ozow validates the request and returns a redirect URL.
2. **Customer completes payment** — You redirect your customer to that URL, where they authenticate with their bank and approve the transfer.
3. **Receive the outcome** — Ozow redirects the customer back to your `SuccessUrl`, `CancelUrl`, or `ErrorUrl` with transaction details in the query string, and simultaneously POSTs a notification to your `NotifyUrl` webhook.

## Base URLs

| Environment | Base URL |
|-------------|----------|
| Staging     | `https://stagingapi.ozow.com` |
| Production  | `https://api.ozow.com` |

Always build and test against the staging environment first, then switch base URL and credentials when you go live.

## Authentication at a glance

Every request requires two credentials you retrieve from your Merchant Dashboard:

- **API Key** — passed in the request body as the `ApiKey` field.
- **Hash** — a SHA-512 signature computed from your request fields and your Private Key, passed as `HashCheck`. Your Private Key is never sent over the wire.

See the [Authentication](/api/payin/authentication) page for the full field order and a code example.

<Note>
  For new integrations, consider the [OneAPI](/api/oneapi/overview) instead. It offers OAuth 2.0 authentication, built-in idempotency, and broader payment method support beyond EFT.
</Note>
